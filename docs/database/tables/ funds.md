# Funds Table Specification

## Purpose

The `funds` table represents money reserved for a specific financial purpose.

A fund is not a bank account.

A fund is a logical allocation of money toward a goal or obligation.

Examples include emergency savings, zakat, future purchases, and other financial objectives.

Funds do not store money directly.

All balances are derived from related transactions.

# Business Rules

- A fund represents a purpose, not an account.
- Money enters a fund through transactions.
- Money leaves a fund through transactions.
- Fund balance is always calculated.
- Users may create unlimited funds.
- Funds may exist without a target amount.
- Closed funds remain available for historical reporting.

# Design Decisions

- Funds are logical reserves.
- Funds are not bank accounts.
- Fund balances are never stored.
- Transactions are the source of truth.
- Historical fund activity remains immutable.
- Goal tracking is optional.

---

# Final Statement

The `funds` table enables goal-oriented financial planning without introducing accounting complexity.

By treating funds as logical allocations instead of separate accounts, the application remains simple while providing powerful long-term savings and planning capabilities.
